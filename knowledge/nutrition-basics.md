# Nutrition Basics — Knowledge Base

## Anti-Inflammatory Eating Principles

### Foods to Prioritize
- **Fatty fish** (salmon, sardines, mackerel) — omega-3s, 2–3x/week
- **Leafy greens** (spinach, kale, arugula) — magnesium, antioxidants
- **Berries** — polyphenols, low glycemic
- **Olive oil** — oleocanthal (NSAID-like effect)
- **Turmeric + black pepper** — curcumin bioavailability
- **Nuts and seeds** (walnuts, chia, flax) — omega-3, fiber
- **Fermented foods** (kefir, yogurt, sauerkraut) — microbiome support
- **Whole grains** (oats, brown rice, quinoa) — fiber, slow glucose
- **Legumes** (lentils, chickpeas) — fiber, plant protein

### Foods to Minimize
- Ultra-processed food (anything with ingredient list you can't read)
- Refined sugar and high-fructose corn syrup
- Industrial seed oils (sunflower, canola in high amounts)
- Alcohol (≥ 2 drinks/day = measurable inflammatory marker rise)
- Red/processed meat daily
- Trans fats

## Macronutrient Targets (General)

| Macro | Target | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Protein | 1.2–2.0g per kg bodyweight | Higher end if training |
| Carbohydrates | 40–55% of calories | Whole grains, vegetables |
| Fat | 25–35% of calories | Prioritize MUFA, omega-3 |
| Fiber | 30–40g/day | Most people get ~15g — big gap |

## Oats — Why They're Special

Oats are a nutritional powerhouse — especially relevant for the fasting protocol.

**Key properties:**
- **Beta-glucan fiber:** soluble fiber, proven to reduce LDL cholesterol, feed gut bacteria
- **Low glycemic index:** slow glucose release, sustained satiety
- **Avenanthramides:** anti-inflammatory antioxidants unique to oats
- **Magnesium, zinc, B vitamins:** broad micronutrient profile
- **Protein:** ~5g per 40g serving (decent plant protein)
- **Gentle on the gut:** ideal for fasting transitions and sensitive digestion

**Best forms:** plain rolled oats (Haferflocken grob), not instant, not flavored

## Hydration

- **Baseline:** 30–35ml per kg bodyweight per day
- **With exercise:** add 500–750ml per hour of activity
- **Fasting:** 2.5–3L/day (higher need as solid food water intake drops)
- **Electrolytes:** add a pinch of sea salt + small amount of mineral-rich water if sweating or fasting

Signs of dehydration that affect performance: headache, brain fog, fatigue — often mistaken for hunger

## Gut Microbiome Basics

Gut health underlies systemic inflammation, immune function, mood, and digestion.

**Support with:**
- Prebiotic fiber (oats, garlic, onion, leek, asparagus)
- Probiotic foods (kefir, plain yogurt, kimchi, sauerkraut, miso)
- Diversity — aim for 30+ different plant foods per week
- Avoid unnecessary antibiotics and artificial sweeteners

**Signs of poor gut health:**
- Bloating, irregular stools, food sensitivities
- Fatigue, brain fog, low mood
- Frequent illness

## Meal Timing for Performance

- **Pre-workout (1–2h before):** small carb + protein meal (banana + yogurt, oats)
- **Post-workout (within 30–60 min):** protein + carbs (crucial for muscle repair)
- **Before bed:** small protein snack improves overnight muscle protein synthesis (e.g., Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)
- **Avoid:** heavy meals within 2–3h of sleep

## Supplements Worth Considering

| Supplement | Evidence | Dose |
|-----------|----------|------|
| Vitamin D3 + K2 | Strong | 2000–4000 IU D3 daily |
| Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) | Strong | 1–2g combined EPA/DHA |
| Magnesium glycinate | Good | 200–400mg/night |
| Creatine monohydrate | Very strong (even for non-athletes) | 3–5g/day |
| Probiotics (multi-strain) | Moderate | 10–50 billion CFU |
| Zinc | Moderate | 15–25mg/day (if deficient) |

**Not necessary but common:** Vitamin B complex, CoQ10, adaptogenic herbs (ashwagandha for stress/cortisol)

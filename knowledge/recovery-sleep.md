# Recovery & Sleep — Knowledge Base

## Sleep as the Foundation

Sleep is the single most impactful recovery lever. No supplement, training protocol, or nutrition strategy outperforms consistent quality sleep.

### Targets
- **Duration:** 7–9 hours (individual variation is real — track energy, not just hours)
- **Consistency:** same wake time daily (including weekends) — anchors the circadian clock
- **Quality markers:** feeling rested within 30 min of waking; stable energy in the morning

---

## Sleep Architecture

| Stage | Duration | Function |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Light sleep (N1/N2) | ~50% of night | Memory consolidation, transition |
| Deep sleep (N3/SWS) | ~20–25% | Physical repair, immune function, HGH release |
| REM sleep | ~20–25% | Emotional processing, learning, motor skill |

**Key insight:** Deep sleep is front-loaded (first half of night). REM is back-loaded. Cutting sleep short kills REM; going to bed late reduces SWS.

## Sleep Hygiene Protocol

### Optimize the Environment
- **Temperature:** 17–19°C bedroom (cooler = deeper sleep)
- **Darkness:** blackout curtains or sleep mask (even small light exposure degrades melatonin)
- **Noise:** earplugs or white noise if needed
- **Phone:** out of the bedroom, or on airplane mode

### Circadian Anchor Habits
- **Morning light:** get outside within 30–60 min of waking for 10+ min (even on cloudy days)
- **No screens:** 60–90 min before sleep (blue light suppresses melatonin)
- **Consistent bedtime:** within 30 min window every night

### Wind-Down Routine (60 min before bed)
- Lower light levels (warm tones only)
- Light stretching or mobility work
- Breathing practice: 4-7-8 breathing or box breathing
- No work emails / news / social media
- Optional: warm bath (raises then drops core temp — powerful sleep signal)

## Recovery Between Workouts

### Active Recovery
- Light walking, swimming, yoga
- Promotes blood flow, reduces DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness)
- Better than complete rest for most people

### Foam Rolling / Self-Myofascial Release
- Technique: slow rolling (not fast), pause on tender spots 20–30s
- Don't roll directly on joint or bony areas
- Best timing: post-workout or evening
- Priority areas for lower back protocol: hip flexors, glutes, TFL, thoracic spine

### Cold/Heat Contrast
- Cold shower post-exercise (2–3 min): reduces inflammation acutely
- Sauna / hot bath (15–20 min): promotes relaxation, GH release
- Not both in the same session if hypertrophy is goal (cold attenuates anabolic signaling)

## Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

HRV = variation in time between heartbeats. High HRV = good recovery. Low HRV = under-recovered or stressed.

**How to use it:**
- Measure each morning upon waking (device: Garmin, Polar, WHOOP, or free app + chest strap)
- If HRV is 10–15% below your baseline: reduce workout intensity, prioritize sleep
- Trend matters more than single readings

## Stress & Cortisol

Chronic stress = chronically elevated cortisol = impaired sleep, fat gain (especially abdominal), muscle breakdown, suppressed immunity.

### Cortisol Management Tools
- **Breathwork:** box breathing (4-4-4-4) — activates parasympathetic in 2–5 min
- **Cold exposure:** trains the nervous system's stress response
- **Exercise:** acute cortisol spike that improves sensitivity and reduces baseline
- **Nature exposure:** 20 min in green space reduces cortisol measurably
- **Ashwagandha:** 300–600mg KSM-66 extract — good evidence for cortisol reduction

## Recovery During Fasting

Special considerations for oat-based fasting periods:
- **Reduce training volume 70–80%** — body is in catabolic/autophagy mode
- **Prioritize sleep** — healing and cellular repair accelerated during fast + sleep
- **No high-intensity exercise**
- **Gentle lower back mobilization is fine on days 3–5** if no dizziness
- **Increase rest time** — expect 1–2h more sleep than normal

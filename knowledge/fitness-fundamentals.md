# Fitness Fundamentals — Knowledge Base

## Training Principles

### Progressive Overload
Gradually increase stress on the body over time — the single most important training principle.
- Increase reps → then sets → then weight → then complexity
- Rule: don't increase more than 10% load per week

### Specificity (SAID Principle)
The body adapts specifically to the demands placed on it.
- Want better lower back? Train the lower back and its stabilizers
- Want aerobic capacity? Do aerobic work

### Recovery = Growth
Gains happen during rest, not during training.
- 48h minimum between same muscle group sessions
- Sleep is the most powerful recovery tool

### Consistency > Intensity
3 moderate sessions per week, every week, beats sporadic heroic efforts.

## Weekly Movement Framework (General)

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Mon | Mobility + light strength (20–30 min) |
| Tue | Walk 30–45 min (Zone 2 cardio) |
| Wed | Strength training (full body, 30–40 min) |
| Thu | Rest or light yoga |
| Fri | Strength or HIIT (20 min) |
| Sat | Longer walk / hike / bike ride |
| Sun | Rest — passive stretching / foam rolling |

Adapt to current fitness level. Start with 3 days if 6 feels overwhelming.

## Zone 2 Cardio — Why It Matters

Zone 2 = conversational pace, 60–70% max HR, you can speak in sentences.

**Benefits:**
- Builds aerobic base and mitochondrial density
- Burns fat as primary fuel
- Reduces cardiovascular risk
- Supports longevity (Dr. Peter Attia research)

**Target:** 150+ min/week cumulative

**Best forms:** brisk walking, light cycling, swimming, light jogging

## Strength Training Basics

### Compound Movements (Priority)
1. Hip hinge (deadlift, RDL, kettlebell swing)
2. Squat pattern (goblet squat, bodyweight squat)
3. Push (push-up, dumbbell press)
4. Pull (row, band pull-apart, lat pull)
5. Carry (farmer carry, suitcase carry — great for core stability)

### Sets / Reps for Beginners
- **Strength:** 3–4 sets × 5–8 reps (heavier)
- **Hypertrophy:** 3–4 sets × 8–15 reps
- **Endurance:** 2–3 sets × 15–25 reps

### Rest Periods
- Strength: 2–3 min
- Hypertrophy: 60–90s
- Endurance/circuit: 30–60s

## Recovery Tools

| Tool | Use Case | Protocol |
|------|----------|---------|
| Sleep | Everything | 7–9 hours, consistent schedule |
| Foam rolling | Tight muscles, fascia | 60–90s per muscle group |
| Cold exposure | Post-exercise inflammation | 2–3 min cold shower |
| Heat (sauna/bath) | Tension, relaxation | 15–20 min, 3x/week |
| Magnesium | Sleep, cramps | 200–400mg at night |
| Stretching | Flexibility, mobility | Hold 30–60s minimum for change |

## Signs of Overtraining

- Persistent fatigue that doesn't resolve with rest
- Performance declining despite training
- Elevated resting heart rate > 5–7bpm above baseline
- Mood disturbance, irritability
- Frequent illness (immune suppression)
- Sleep disruption

**Fix:** reduce volume 40–60% for 1–2 weeks. Prioritize sleep and nutrition.

## HIIT Protocol (Beginner-Friendly)

**Format:** 20s work / 40s rest × 8 rounds (Tabata variant)

**Exercises to rotate:**
- Step jacks or jumping jacks
- Modified mountain climbers
- Glute bridge pulses
- Push-up (knee or full)
- Side shuffles

**Total time:** ~13 min
**Frequency:** 1–2x per week max (high neural cost)

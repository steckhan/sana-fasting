# Lower Back Mobilization — Knowledge Base

## Anatomy Context

The lower back (lumbar spine, L1–L5) is the most common site of chronic pain. Primary causes:
- **Tight hip flexors** (psoas, iliacus) — from prolonged sitting
- **Weak glutes** — shifts load to lumbar extensors
- **Inhibited deep core** (multifidus, transversus abdominis)
- **Poor thoracic mobility** — lumbar compensates
- **Disc compression** — from sedentary posture

## Daily 5-Minute Routine (Foundation)

Do every morning or after prolonged sitting.

### 1. Cat-Cow (2 min)
- On all fours, spine neutral
- Inhale → drop belly, lift chest and tailbone (cow)
- Exhale → round spine to ceiling, tuck chin (cat)
- Pace: slow and controlled, 8–10 reps
- **Goal:** Restore segmental mobility, wake up spinal extensors

### 2. Supine Knee-to-Chest (1 min)
- Lie on back, pull both knees to chest
- Hold 30s, then slow circles with knees (10 each direction)
- **Goal:** Decompression, release lumbar tension

### 3. Supine Spinal Twist (1 min)
- Lie flat, bring one knee across body, arms wide, gaze away
- Hold 20–30s each side
- **Goal:** Thoracolumbar fascia release, rotational mobility

### 4. Child's Pose (1 min)
- Knees wide, arms extended, sink hips toward heels
- Breathe into lower back, allow passive elongation
- **Goal:** Passive decompression, hip flexor stretch

## 20-Minute Deeper Session (3x per week)

### Phase 1: Activation (5 min)
- 90/90 Hip Switches × 10 slow reps
- Dead Bug × 10 reps (5 per side) — keep lower back pressed to floor
- Glute Bridge × 15 reps, 2-second hold at top

### Phase 2: Mobility (8 min)
- World's Greatest Stretch × 5 each side
- Hip Flexor Kneeling Lunge Stretch — 60s each side
- Pigeon Pose — 60s each side (or Figure-4 supine)
- Thoracic Extension over foam roller — 10 breaths at 3 levels

### Phase 3: Strengthening (5 min)
- Bird Dog × 10 reps per side (controlled, don't rotate pelvis)
- Plank — 3 × 20–30s (progress to 60s)
- Side-Lying Clamshells × 15 each side (glute med activation)

### Phase 4: Cool-Down (2 min)
- Child's pose — 30s
- Supine twist — 30s each
- Diaphragmatic breathing — 5 deep breaths

## Exercise Progressions

| Level | Focus | Add |
|-------|-------|-----|
| Week 1–2 | Pain-free movement, body awareness | Foundation routine only |
| Week 3–4 | Add deeper session 2x/week | Glute bridges, bird dog |
| Week 5–8 | Build strength | Romanian deadlifts (light), hip hinge practice |
| Week 9+ | Load progression | Deadlifts, weighted hip thrusts |

---

## Red Flags — Stop and Seek Medical Help

- Pain radiating down leg (sciatic symptoms)
- Numbness or tingling in feet/toes
- Bladder or bowel changes
- Pain after rest that's worse than before
- Sharp pain during any movement

---

## Lifestyle Recommendations

- **Sitting:** Max 45 min unbroken, stand/walk for 5 min
- **Sleeping position:** Side-lying with pillow between knees, or back with pillow under knees
- **Footwear:** Avoid flat, zero-support shoes all day
- **Hydration:** Discs are ~80% water — stay hydrated
- **Heat therapy:** 10–15 min moist heat before morning routine loosens tight tissue
